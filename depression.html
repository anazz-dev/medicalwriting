Case Study: Addressing the Gap in Arabic Mental Health Communication Through A Successful Cross-Platform Campaign on Depression

Published on: July 01, 2023

Introduction

This case study presents an effectively executed medical communication campaign focused on raising awareness about depression within the Arabic-speaking community. Despite the high prevalence of mental health issues, including depression, there remains a significant gap in addressing these topics in Arabic language and culture. In response to this, the campaign was conceptualized to not only provide information but also dispel stigma around mental health in the Arab world.

Campaign Objective

The central aim was to increase understanding and awareness of depression amongst Arabic speakers. By presenting this content in Arabic, the campaign sought to address the existing linguistic and cultural gap in mental health communication, thereby breaking down barriers to awareness, understanding, and treatment of depression.

Campaign Execution

The campaign leveraged the power of video content and social media platforms, creating an informative and engaging video in Arabic, detailing depression, its symptoms, treatment options, and avenues for help. The video was shared on YouTube, and further promoted on Facebook, ensuring a wide reach within the target demographic.

Results

The campaign's success was evident in the level of engagement and reach achieved. On YouTube, the video attracted 2,793 views, 92 likes, and accumulated 280 hours of watch time, reflecting both substantial reach and viewer engagement. The 43 comments left on the video demonstrated an encouraging level of audience interaction, signifying the effectiveness of the video in provoking thought and discussion.

On Facebook, the campaign maintained a high level of success. The video received 368 likes and 55 comments, demonstrating active user engagement. Furthermore, it was reshared 19 times, allowing the campaign to penetrate deeper into the target audience's networks and extend its impact beyond the initial viewer base.

Conclusion

This medical communication campaign exemplifies the successful use of YouTube and Facebook to spread awareness about depression within the Arabic-speaking community. The results indicate not only a high level of engagement but also suggest a much-needed shift in attitudes and understanding about mental health in the Arab world.

The campaign's success underscores the importance and potential effectiveness of targeted, culturally-considerate mental health initiatives in addressing the existing gaps in mental health communication in the Arabic-speaking community. It sets a precedent for future initiatives aiming to tackle other mental health issues in the region.
